Question: How long does the blood donation process take?

Answer: The whole blood donation process takes between 45 and 60 minutes. Whereas blood donation with apheresis (separation of double red blood cell units, platelets and plasma) takes between one and a half and two hours. And the time it takes for a double donation of red blood cells is approximately 30 minutes longer than the time for a whole blood donation

Question: How often can I donate blood?

Answer: You can donate whole blood at least every 84 days. Plasma donors can donate every 28 days. Platelet donors can donate every eight days, up to 24 times over a 12-month period. Double red blood cell donors can donate every 168 days

Question: At what age can I start donating blood?

Answer: Eligible 16-year-olds can donate blood. Donors must have parental or guardian consent and weigh at least 110 pounds (about 50 kilograms).

Question: Can I donate if I have a cold, flu or fever?

Answer: No. To be able to donate, you must be free of cold, flu and fever symptoms on the day of the donation.

Question: From whom each blood type receives new blood?

If your blood type is Rh+ positive, you receive from Rh+ positive and Rh-negative, taking into account the ABO type.

If your blood type is Rh-negative, you receive only Rh-negative blood, with regard to the ABO type.

Blood type A receives only from A and O, taking into account the Rh factor.

B blood group receives only from B and O, taking into account the Rh factor.

AB blood type receives all types, taking into account the Rh factor.

O blood type receives from O only, taking into account the Rh factor.

Rh-negative O blood is given to all types of blood groups.

O-positive blood group is given to all types of Rh-positive blood groups.
